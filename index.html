Tuffy Licciardi Issa

I am Tuffy Licciardi Issa, an economics student at FEA-USP interested in applied microeconomics, econometrics, and development economics. My research focuses on crime, labor markets, and educational constraints in low- and middle-income countries, with an emphasis on microeconomic analysis and policy evaluation. This website brings together my research, teaching materials, curriculum vitae, and contact information.

My academic interests have been shaped by a multicultural background and a strong interest in combining economic theory, empirical methods, and social policy analysis. I also care deeply about transparent and reproducible research, and I regularly share my workflows in R and LaTeX.

About

I am an aspiring economist motivated by questions about opportunity, inequality, and the institutional forces that shape individual outcomes. My interest in economics grew from a broader curiosity about how people respond to constraints, incentives, and public policy. Over time, this led me to applied research at the intersection of labor, crime, education, and development.

My academic perspective has also been shaped by a multicultural background and by interests that extend beyond economics alone. I care deeply about clear thinking, rigorous empirical work, and reproducible research practices, and I regularly use and share workflows in R and LaTeX.

Articles and Bulletins

Avanços recentes da inteligência artificial: impactos trabalhistas, sociais e regulatórios [PT-BR] with José Paulo Zeetano Chahad. Jornal da USP, Feb. 21, 2024.

Overview article on how artificial intelligence is reshaping labor markets, skill demand, inequality, social inclusion, and the broader regulatory debate.

O Estágio Atual da Inteligência Artificial: História, Impactos no Mercado de Trabalho, Aspectos Sociais e Alternativas de Regulação (Parte I – Aspectos Históricos e Impactos no Mercado de Trabalho) [PT-BR] with José Paulo Zeetano Chahad. Informações FIPE, v. 520, Jan. 2024.

Review article tracing the historical development of artificial intelligence and examining its effects on employment, job matching, and the evolving demand for skills in the labor market.

O estágio atual da inteligência artificial: impactos no mercado de trabalho, aspectos sociais e alternativas de regulação (Parte II – aspectos distributivos e alternativas de regulação) [PT-BR] with José Paulo Zeetano Chahad. Informações FIPE, v. 521, Feb. 2024.

Review article on the distributive and social consequences of artificial intelligence, with particular attention to regulation, public policy, and the role of collective bargaining in technological transition.

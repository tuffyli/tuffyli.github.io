About

I am interested in development economics, with an emphasis on microeconomic analysis of crime, labor markets, and education constraints in low- and middle-income countries. I seek to combine economic theory, econometrics, and social policy evaluation. This academic curiosity was largely shaped by my multicultural background and multidisciplinary interests. My family roots in Italy and Lebanon also contributed to my uncommon name for a Brazilian. I share my reproducible research workflows in R and LaTeX.

Research Interests

Applied Microeconomics

My research centers on applied microeconomics, with a focus on labor economics, policy evaluation, crime economics, education, and development economics. I leverage causal inference methods, drawing on Brazilian administrative data to uncover insights into economic behaviors and policy impacts in low- and middle-income contexts.
